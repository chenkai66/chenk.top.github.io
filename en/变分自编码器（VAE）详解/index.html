Variational Autoencoder (VAE): A Detailed Guide

In generative modeling, the Variational Autoencoder (VAE) combines representation learning (autoencoders) with probabilistic latent-variable modeling. A VAE can both reconstruct data and generate new samples by learning a distribution over a latent space. The key engineering idea is the reparameterization trick, which makes sampling differentiable and enables end-to-end training with backpropagation.

What is an Autoencoder?

An Autoencoder (AE) can be seen as a learned compression system. It consists of:

  • Encoder: maps an input to a low-dimensional latent vector .
  • Decoder: maps back to a reconstruction .

Training is unsupervised: the model learns by minimizing a reconstruction loss between and .

¶Key properties

  1. Data-dependent compression: an AE compresses well on data similar to what it was trained on, and poorly outside that distribution.
  2. Lossy compression: information is inevitably lost when mapping from high to low dimension; the decoder learns to restore what it can.

¶Common use cases

  • Denoising: train the AE to reconstruct clean data from noisy inputs.
  • Nonlinear dimensionality reduction: similar goal to PCA but with nonlinear mappings.
  • Data generation (limited): by manipulating latent vectors, you can decode variations—though a vanilla AE does not give you a principled sampling distribution.

What is a VAE?

A Variational Autoencoder (VAE) is a generative model. Instead of mapping to a single deterministic latent vector, it learns a distribution over latent variables. The typical choice is a Gaussian posterior parameterized by the encoder:

¶AE vs VAE (core differences)

  1. Objective

    • AE: learn a compact representation for reconstruction/denoising/feature extraction.
    • VAE: learn a latent distribution that supports sampling to generate new data.

  2. Latent space

    • AE: deterministic latent vector.
    • VAE: stochastic latent variable with a learned posterior distribution.

  3. Sampling

    • AE: no principled sampling distribution.
    • VAE: can sample from a prior (usually standard normal) and decode into data space.

¶Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO)

VAE maximizes the ELBO:

$$
\log p_\theta(x) \ge \mathbb{E}{q\phi(z\mid x)}[\log p_\theta(x\mid z)] - \mathrm{KL}(q_\phi(z\mid x),|,p(z))
$$

  • The expectation term is a reconstruction term.
  • The KL term regularizes the posterior to stay close to the prior.

Variational inference steps (conceptual)

  1. Encode
  2. Sample via a differentiable transformation (reparameterization)
  3. Decode
  4. Optimize ELBO via backpropagation
