Graph Representations Overview

7月 18, 2022 发布在 Graph Neural Networks

Paper Wasserstein Weisfeiler-Lehman Graph Kernels(cited 110 times) proposed the graph Wasserstein distance and how kernels can be derived from it, and also proposed a seemly out-of-date node embedding method. The specific node embedding method depends on whether the representation of a node is continuous according to its labels or features (the authors seem to be a little confused on these two distinctions). For the labelled non-attributed graph: \[\ell^{h+1}(v)=\operatorname{hash}\left(\ell^{h}(v), \mathcal{N}^{h}(v)\right)\] While for the continuous attributes graph: \[a^{h+1}(v)=\frac{1}{2}\left(a^{h}(v)+\frac{1}{\operatorname{deg}(v)} \sum_{u \in \mathcal{N}(v)} w((v, u)) \cdot a^{h}(u)\right)\] The summary of graph Wasserstein distance is shown as follows:
