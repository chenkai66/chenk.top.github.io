Learning rate (LR) is the training knob that most often decides whether optimization converges, crawls, or blows up—and it’s also the easiest to mis-tune if you don’t have the right mental model. This post builds that model from the simplest quadratic case to modern large-scale training recipes: why stability has a hard bound, why schedules help, and how warmup / cosine / WSD / schedule-free ideas fit together. You’ll get intuition, the minimum math that explains the behavior, and concrete tuning workflows you can actually follow.

Learning rate (LR) is the single knob that most often decides whether training learns, crawls, or blows up. If you want one mental picture, keep this:

  • Parameters are your position.
  • Loss is altitude (lower is better).
  • Gradient is the downhill direction you can sense.
  • Learning rate is how big a step you take.
